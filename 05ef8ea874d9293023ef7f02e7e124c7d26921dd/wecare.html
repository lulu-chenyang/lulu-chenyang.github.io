Methodology

Needfinding Plan

User type


The new interface design will be aimed towards 2 primary users.


1. Parents:

Seniors look for assistance to maintain a healthy lifestyle that are comfortable with sharing some of their medical data. Demographic Information: These are parents of adult children, with the average age ranging from 44 to 78 years based on the average birth age[2] + 18 and the average age of death.[3] Expertise: Senior users will be closer to novice or inexperienced users in expertise due to their smartphone adoption rate[4] to be about half of younger adults. Motivations: The parent will desire assistance in maintaining their health choices and activities. A secondary motivation will be those looking to improve inter-family communications.


2. Children of the Parents:

These are children who would like to take a more active role in helping to encourage their parents towards a healthy lifestyle. Demographic Information: The children will be adults typically ranging from 18 – 40. Expertise: With the majority of young adults using smartphones[4], the average child of a parent will be in-between an average user’s expertise level up to an expert level. Motivations: The children will be those wanting to ensure that their parents' health is a high priority.

Survey

13 questions are generated for an internet-based survey to identify who are the users, what are the users' goals, what are the users' tasks, what is the context of the tasks, and what do the users need.

Confirmation bias will be limited by avoiding leading question, adding question allowing neutral responses, and reviewing survey responses as a group. Voluntary response bias will be difficult to overcome due to the participation credit offered to participants.

Key take-aways
  • The majority of participants are in the age range 26-35. This correlates well with the national average master student’s age of 33[ref #1].
  • A large majority participants were interested in their parents health and an equally large majority don’t live in the same city.
  • The majority of participants talked to their parents at least bi-weekly with most asking about their parents health around a similar amount.
  • A smartphone would cover almost all participants parents with personal computer covering about half, with the smartphone covering all of the major communication types: Phone call, social media, video call, texting.
  • Common smart monitors and wearables like step, heart, blood pressure, and glucose aligns well the expected participants’ chronic diseases or unhealthy behavior. These same devices cover the information participants would like to monitor and would be willing to purchase for their parents.
  • Majority would be interested in seeing medical report health data.

    • Interview

    8 interviews are conducted to seniors' fitness behavior, health-related communication with their children, and usage of technology to inform design decision in later phases.

    During this needfinding, social desirability bias, confirmation bias and observer bias are possible. Confirmation bias will be limited by scripting down questions . Social desirability bias will be limited by disclosing results would be kept anonymous. Observer bias will be constrained by reviewing the interview responses as a group.

    Key take-aways
  • The ages of the 8 interviewees fall between 50 to 72, which are in the predefined age range 44 to 78 years old. And their children range between 25 to 44, which 93.75% fall into the predefined age range 18 to 40. More than 62.5% of the seniors and their children do not in live the same city.
  • 75% of the seniors communicate with their children at least once a week. 50% communicate with their children every day. The communication platform used by seniors and their kids fall into 2 categories: traditional communication (text, phone) and app-based communication (message, video call).
  • The topic among seniors and their kids are varied. Food, work and life are the three major topics.
  • 75% of the seniors do not have any chronic disease. Their understanding of healthy lifestyle are different.
  • Most of the seniors regular take medical examination. However the frequency is varied upon their health condition.
  • 87.5% of the seniors use smartphones. Seniors who are more than 60 have tough time using smartphones and apps.
  • Social, news, and entertainment are three of the most used app types by the seniors.
  • More than 62.5% are willing to share upon request. The major concerns of not sharing health or activities are afraid their children to worry about them.
  • All (100%) of the users feel happy when kids are caring about their health.
  • All (100%) of the seniors would like to have more conversation with their kids.
  • Eat healthy, enough exercise, and propriate relaxation are three of the common understanding of healthy lifestyle.
  • More than 62.5% of the seniors thought they have a healthy lifestyle.
  • 50% of the seniors are comfortable wearing wearables.
  • 62.5% experience frustration about smartphone
  • 75% willing to wear wearable
  • Great suggestion on rewarding seniors information about their hobbies.
