Stay Well

Create a Holistic Solution for Healthy Travel Experience

2018 - present (2 years)

Stay Well is a program created for healthy travel experiences. Collaborated with famous hotel brands like MGM, Marriott, Wyndham, etc., Stay Well minimizes the impact that travel has on humans through curated products and personalized program.

How Stay Well Work

Stay Well team bought products from manufacturers, pre-configurated and re-packed, then shipped to hotels. Stay Well team flew to the hotel to commission, install the product, and train hotel staff how to use the products.

Challenges

A product consists of products

Stay Well consists of light, air purifier, aromatherapy, mobile app, etc. It also includes the complicated product scouting and evaluation, which envloves 3 teams.

The website and App are lack of maintenance

01. The Stay Well App and website haven’t been updated for more than 2 years. The design and content are not able to represent the current value proposition.
02. More then 9 Stay Well Apps exist on Apple App Store. None of them are rated more than 4 stars.
03. The jet lag relief concept was innovative when it was developed 9 years ago, however, the design and algorithm fall behind its competitors.

Promote the product in different country

A great business opportunity required us to promote the product to people in different country, language, and climate, etc. and with potential different needs.

The product evaluation report can't bring direct revenue

Delos has generated thousands of product reports when developing the health and wellness product ecosystem. Everyone see the health value of the report. However, those reports haven't generated any direct revenue.

Kitckoff

Look into the User Information

Since Stay Well is a B2B business and currently we don't have direct connection with hotel guest, we don't have direct user information.

After talking with Stay Well team and hotel management staff, I identified 4 users of the website: business travellars, families, jet setters and hotel owners (management companies).

By looking into the demographic information in Las Vegas (where we most collaborated hotels located), I found that the majority of the visitors are married, with an annual household income of $40,000 and more, 40 years old or older. Most of them are repeat visitors.
