How Stay Well Work

Delos bought product from manufacturers, pre-configurated and re-packed it in Delos Office, then shipped to hotels. Delos staff flew to the hotel to commission, install the product, and train hotel staff how to use the product.

Challenges

A product with many different products

The biggest challenges for Stay Well is that it's a package of products, which include light, air purifier, aromatherapy, app, web, WeChat mini program, product evaluation reports, etc. At the same time, though the program continues bring in a big revenue, the shipping and labour are the cost center of the program.

The website and app are lack of maintenance and iteration

01. The Stay Well App and website were first released 4 - 5 years ago and haven’t been updated for more than 2 years. The design and content are not able to represent the current value proposition.
02. More then 9 Stay Well app exist on Apple App Store. The number of rating is less than 5 while the average star is 3.
03. The jet lag relief concept was innovative when it was developed 9 years ago, due to lack of maintenance and iteration, the feature fall behind its competitors.

Product Evaluation Report can't bring direct revenue

Delos has generated thousands of product reports when developing the health and wellness product ecosystem and helping our clients find products. Everyone see the health value of the report. However, those reports haven't generated any direct revenue.

Need to promote the product in different market

A great business opportunity required us to adapt the product to people in different country, language, and climate, etc.

Look into the User Information

Since Stay Well is a B2B business and currently we don't have direct connection with hotel guest, we don't have direct user information.

After talking with Stay Well team and hotel management staff, I identified 4 users of the website: business travellars, families, jet setters and hotel owners (management companies).

By looking into the demographic information in Las Vegas (where we most collaborated hotels located), I found that the majority of the visitors are married, with an annual household income of $40,000 and more, 40 years old or older. Most of them are repeat visitors.
