The original thermostat control panel

When Delos started to promot DARWIN in US market in 2018, I was tasked to lead the redesign of product. Before that, I was responsible for product innovation.

Challenges

Localization
+ Hardware Meets Software
+ Multiple Product line

01. From Australia to the US, we are not sure if people will buy in the concept.
02. The UI is out of date and not compatible with the hardware used in the US market.
03. There are multiple product lines with different layout and design patterns harmed the brand integrity.
04. According to the clients in Australia, people are hard to perceive the improvement of their wellness and living environment overtime.

Kickoff

Prove the Concept

I conducted a market survey along side a behavior scientist and a marketing expert. In the survey, we asked people’s knowledge on the connection between their health and the indoor environment, what do they excited about, what do they trust, what health-based features should go into a smart home.

1000+ responses to user survey were collected. People overwhelmingly believed that their home can impact their health and well-being. Air quality, environmental remediation, and sleep are the top 3 features people are most excited about.

Discover

Getting Insights from Beta

Worked with 2 PMs and 3 engineers, we shipped the DARWIN kit to 20 Beta users and asked them to fill out surveys and every 2 weeks over 8 weeks testing period. And conducted an interview at week 10. Our goals were to understand the challenges they faced and the workarounds they employed when they used in home settings.

01. DARWIN improved users’ awareness of air quality. But users wanted more explaination about the pollutants, the health benifits of the system, and to be notified when air quality is not good.

02. Testers want more control to set the duration of their Dawn Simulation, as well increased dimming capabilities across all experiences.

03. Testers want the icons on home screen to be clickable. Testers with Alexa and Google Home used voice control and physical switch in most case.

04. Testers felt hard to find certain functions (e.g. air filter status, schedule a Wake up Experience, schedule a Sleep Experience).

05. Testers are confused about the thermostat control panel.

Discovery

Not a Simple UX/UI Problem

I was surprised that more than 60% of the testers are unlikely to recommend the DARWIN system to their friend and colleague. The dissatisfaction majorly came from 3 aspect:
01 Poor connectivity and integration with hardware
02 Lack of simplicity and consistency in the UX
03 No personalization in the algorithm

It's not a Simple UX/UI Problem. To make a delightful IoT product and improve satisfaction, we need team effort.

Deeper Insight

Workshop

With the support from the leadership, I organized a series of workshops with engineers, scientists, PMs, marketing, etc. We empathized a typical user (Linda, identified from the demographic data we collected in the survey) and her pain points, mapped out her journeys when interacts with DARWIN, and innovated on ways to improve her user experience.

All stakeholders voted to identify the key pain points of Linda and discussed the opportunities for improvement.

Dive Deep Into the Information Architecture & Design System

After mapped out the existing information architecture, I found there are many unnecessary and complicated navigation steps. For example, the filter status feature is under the user profile sections.

Revise the Design System

A series of design critiques was organized to evaluate the current design system. All the existing components are studied and adjusted to a 4/8 point grid following the design guide.

Solution

Align the UI with hardware functionality

The thermostat in DARWIN home has 3 different mode: Cooling, Heating and Auto. The original thermostat control panel was not designed for the smart thermostat.

Thermostat Design Update V1

During the interview, I realize that even experienced users had trouble distinguishing cooling/heating mode with setting cooling/heating set point.

Users' Major Confusions:

  • Heating and setting cooling setpoint

  • Control slider and control button

  • Where to change the mode

Instead of using a down arrow for users to change mode, a mode picker was used to give user more indication about the 3 modes. All of the users like the new design, and satisfied about its learnability and the ease of use.

Monthly Wellness Summary Communicates the Performance and Health Benifits

Monthly Report is another feature on the product roadmap. Worked with a lot of ambiguity, I started with analyzing the data visualization method used in monthly report of the other apps

The length of the performance report varies upon product. The longest one is within 3 scrolls. The common techniques used in reports including data distribution, comparison(with other users), achievement, etc.

The goal of the performance report plays a key role to determine the metrics, content, layout and length of the report. Worked with PMs, engineers and executives, I set the goals of the project as followed.

Business Goal: Improve users' satisfaction and user retention rate of the app; educate users on how to enhance DARWIN experience or better use products.


Design Goal: Highlight the efforts of the product users paid for and help users benchmark their status and find the solotions to improve by one click.

With the goal clearly defined, I organize a series of brainstorming sessions with a working group

The team came up with different storys we might used in the report. Then I worked with the engineers to evaluate the feasibiltiy of each case and the availability of data, and summarized and illustrated those storys by the following design.

Make Checking & Replacing Filter Easier

The goal of the design is to make user feels "in control” of their connected home ecosystem. At the same time, DARWIN should enable users to learn about the air filter(s) they have in their home and what action(s) they need to take to maintain the filters in their home. Furthermore, DARWIN should help users understand their purchase decisions on the e-commerce website.

The HEPA filter in DARWIN system contains 3 major parts and their lifespans vary:

  • Foam Pre-Filter: 3 to 4 months (washable)
  • HEPA Filter: 2 to 5 years
  • Inner VOC Filter: 12 months

Display the filter with Skeuomorphic visualization. Maping the digital element to the physical object in order to provide user more information, and help them feel more confident identify the part needs to be washed or replaced.

Build a Testbed to Faciliate the Research & Product Innovation

DARWIN X, a testing platform was developed to deploy innovative features on the product roadmap and test the accuracy of the algorithm. It was also expanded from one flagship site into 20+ homes across the country, facilitating the user interviews and survey to test users' interaction with both hardware and software. The DARWIN X laid a solid foundation of the Alpha and Beta Test.
