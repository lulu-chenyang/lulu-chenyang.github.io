UX Research

20+ user interviews are conducted to test users' understanding of the content, grouping, and the navigation of the app. We targeted to have 90% of the tester successfully find where to check their profile, make selections, schedule events, play soundtracks and check their status.

The result shows that the design gave users a great first impression. Some users even thought it exceeded their expectations. Almost all of the designs meet the target, except for: back, navigagte to home screen, and repeat a session

However, users are confused about the difference between "Library" and "Collection" on the app. More than half of the users thought that "My Journal" did not represent its content.

A new prototype was created in a very short time based on the feedback collected during the first interview.

A new idea was brought up during the stakeholder meeting: full-screen background may look more intuitive, hiding the time options under a timer icon may make the design look more simple and clean.

Another round of user interview was organized. 90% of the users are able to complete all the tasks.

The younger generation, especially Spotify users, learned how to use a minibar to play, pause, get back to the full play screen, and exit the minibar faster than the older users. Almost all interviewees liked the convenience of the minibar.

All users felt the Status screen met their expectations, but the icons (infographic) made some of them confused.

In general, users are satisfied with the look and feel of the app. Users felt it was easy to navigate and find things. With the second round of revision, users' ratings on the look and feel increased by 0.2, and rating on ease of use increased by 0.15.

Takeaways

During interviews, users also pointed out a couple of good suggestions, such as adding a short tutorial for first-time users, adding a quick survey, "Do you feel relaxed?" when users finished a mindful session. They also recommend improving the user engagement strategies on Status and Achievement Screen.

Users are the center of the design and the source of inspiration. The feedback from the users will be designed and implemented in the next update.
