Meditation app and products help alleviate stress and improve well-being

Mindbreaks program transforms wellness rooms by utilizing evidence-based products (dynamic lighting, noise-isolationg headphone, air purification, biophilia, etc.) and programming to deliver short, effective, and restorative breaks.

Challenges

Mindbreaks app is originally a tablet app, which is a part of the office wellness program. As new business opportunity came in, business development and marketing team reached out out to UX team and request to update the app. After conducting comprehansive market research and organizing a couple rounds of stakeholder workshop, my team decided to redesign the app and promote the app in mass market with a focusing on the residents of student housing.

Working Process

Prototype

Starting from customer's need, the first version of the prototype is designed based on comprehensive market analysis and a series of stakeholder workshops and brainstormings. Top 30 "wellness" app in Apple Store were analyzed. Users tasks were abstracted after the workshops.

For this younger audience like students, personalization is key. A major characteristic of the Generation Y demographic is the idea of extreme customization of products and services. Based on the competitive analysis, we saw the opportunity to provide personalized content and status tracking function to increase the user engagement.

UX Research

20+ user interviews are conducted to test users' understanding of the the content, grouping, and the navigation of the app. Making selections, scheduling events, checking profile and checking status are the major task that users are expected to perform without barrier.

Result shows that the design gave users a great first impression. Some users even thought it exceeded thier expectation. Almost all users successfully accomplished: filter search, favorite, check favorited content, navigate to previous screen, navigate to landing screen, schedule mindful minutes, find a meditation, repeat a the meditation, etc.

However, users are confused about the difference between "Library" and "Collection" on the app. More than half of the users thought that "My Journal" did not represents its content.

New prototype was developed in a very short time based on the feedback collected during the first interview.

New idea is brought up during the stakeholder meeting: full-screen background may look more intuitive, hiding the time options under a timer icon may make the design look more simple and clean.

Another around of interviews are organized to test the new ideas and design revision. New terminology "Experience", "You" garnered a better reaction than the previously-used "Library" and "My Journal". Some users didn't notice the newly designed mini track bar at the bottom until prompted.

During the interview, younger generation, especially Spotify users, learned how to use mini track bar to play, pause, get back to the full play screen, and exit the mini bar faster than the older users. Almost all interviewees like the convienence of mini bar. But some of them are concerned they won't use it during guided meditation.

All users felt the Status screen met their expectations, but the icons (infographic) made some of them confused. Half of the users thought the information on Achievement screen is helpful; but motivation for user to use this function needs to be further designed.

In general, users are satisfied with the look and feel of the app. The image selection is the key factor that impacted users opinion. Users felt it was easy to navigate and find things. With the second round of revision, users's rating on look and feel increased by 0.2 and rating on ease of use increased by 0.15.

During interviews, users also pointed out a couple of good suggestions, such as: adding a short tutorial fro the first time users, adding a quick survey at the end of the track "Do you feel relaxed?", improve the user engagement strategies on status and achievement. However, the design and development of those feature has to be implement in phase 2 because of the tight schedule and time limitation.
