The Unique Challenge

As a wellness intelligence platform, DARWIN would like to improve the health & well-being of people by leveraging the places they live, work, and play… However, when software meets hardwares, challenges are raising from connectivity to consistency and usability, etc. Most of the smart home ecosystems are disconnected, experiences are siloed, while people want their homes to do more for them. Furthermore, from users’ perspective there’s also too much noise & complexity within the wellness space to know exactly what to do.

Working Process

Bring in design thinking working process to the team. Organized multiple stake holder workshop, lead user interviews and surveys, collected and analyzed user feedback in alpha/beta testing. Bridge the gap between scientific research and product development.

Discovery

Market research is conducted. 1000+ survey responses are collected. The age of respondents range from 21 - 80 years old with equal gender distribution. Basic census information is collected, which includes income, education, political views, etc. The survey asked the familiarity with smart-home technology, knowledge and familiarity of connected wellness and digital health, knowledge of connection between indoor environment and health and well-being, what health-based features should go into a smart home, what are people excited about, what do people trust, that would people pay for, barriers from purchasing, justification in purchasing, etc. Persona is drawned from the respondents.

People overwhelming believe that their home can impact their health and well-being. Air quality, environmental remediation, and sleep are existing features people are most excited about

Monthly Report

Goal

The goal of the feature is to provide DARWIN users with a summary of interpreted data reflecting the performance of Darwin, and in cases providing helpful advice that prompts interaction with the system.

The reports will supplement and not replicate information that provided to users through the Darwin App dashboard.

Competitive Analysis

Comparisons, Aggregations, Distributions, Achievement, Advice
Prototype

Users will feel most satified when they perceive that they are getting value out of Darwin and getting updates of the progress. The content of the report must be considered useful, interesting and nonrepetitive to the user. Two alternatives below were purposed as a starting point to further discussion.

Open Window Notification

Proper ventilation to get enough fresh air is essential for good indoor air quality (especially CO2) and thereby for people’s health. However, people can’t notice pollutants in the air, and they are not aware of how simple actions like opening windows can remediate CO2 and keep their indoor air clean. Delos does not have an automated fresh air solution and therefore needs people to open windows to keep air fresh.

Product Design

User interview shows that users expected to have the solution when they are told that their air quality is poor. They also expected more interaction with the system.

With the requirement of minimum change to the existing UI element. An open window notification with indoor pollutant, action items, outdoor temperature and humidity, is added to system.

Thermostat Control

The thermostat in DARWIN home has 3 different mode: Cooling, Heating and Auto. The Auto mode is the most complicated mode since it has a cooling set point, and heating set point. During the interview, I realize that even experienced users had trouble distinguishing cooling/heating mode with setting cooling/heating set point.

User interview shows that users were confused about the 2 control methods of the heating and cooling setpoint, control slider and control button. Some of the users have hard time to find where to change the mode.

The 2 control methods are combined in the new design. Instead of using a down arrow for users to change mode, a mode picker was used to give user more indication about the 3 mode. All of the users like the new design, and satisfied about the learnability and the ease of use.

Filter Replacement Reminder

Air filter can help to ensure that the air you breathe is clean and free of harmful pollutants. Proper care and maintenance of the filter system will ensure years of servcie. The HEPA filter in DARWIN system contains 3 major parts and their lifespans vary:

  • Foam Pre-Filter: 3 to 4 months (washable)
  • HEPA Filter: 2 to 5 years
  • Inner VOC Filter: 12 months
Goal

The goal of the design is to make user feels "in control” of their connected home ecosystem (in this case, as it relates to air filters). At the same time, DARWIN should enable users to learn about the air filter(s) they have in their home and what action(s) they need to take to maintain the filters in their home. Furthermore, DARWIN should help users understand their purchase decisions on the e-commerce website.

Alternative 1

Display the filter with Skeumorphic visualization. Maping the digital element to the physical object in order to provide user more information, and help them feel more confident identify the part needs to be washed or replaced.

Alternative 2

Display the filter status with a classical meter visualization. The visualization is better alaigned with the existing elements on the dashboard.
