Product Design

Moodbox

Unique Challenges
Connecting devices in hotel is very different from connecting devices at home due to privacy issue. Usually, hotels provide different internet for internal use and guests. In addition, the user of the control system, the guest is always changing. The design of the control interface should consider the 3 different users: guest, commission staff and cleaning staff. It should also be combined with hotel cleaning protocol and staff training.
Product Design
User Study and Prototype
Primary user

- Hotel guest

The project was kicked off by a series of stakeholder workshops and a comprehensive review of the users feedback, sales notes and market review. Worked closely with hotel manager, interier designer, Stay Well team. 5 main users' tasks, including controling the lights, setting up Dawn simulation, experiencing Dawn Simulation, reading introduction about Stay Well, changing system setttings, had been identified. Storyboards, wireframes was developed for hotel guests interface based on the users' tasks.

Secondary user

- Cleaning staff

The unique part for hotel setting is that guest is important but different almost every day. Cleaning staff needs to rest the Dawn Simulation of the Moodbox everyday so that the guest won't be waken up by previous guest's settings. The reset function needs to be easy access and properly communicate with cleaning staff.

Thirdary user

- Commissioning staff

To provide hotel guests smoothly operation experiences, a lot of work need to be done behind that by commissioning staff. They need to use the system to connect each light bulb to the system and pair specific experience to each light bulb. By carefully studying the commissioning documents, working with lighting expert, and talking with commissioning staff, a series of wireframes are developed.
